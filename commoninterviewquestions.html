Question Goal How to Respond
Tell me about yourself. The interviewer wants you to introduce yourself, so share a brief summary of your career trajectory and relevant experience. Describe the key experiences in your career that qualify you for the position. Avoid diclosing age or going too far back in your history, and keep it focused on your professional life.
Why are you interested in our company? Employers want to see that you've done the research on the company and that it's a place where you could build your career. Before your interview, browse the company's website and social media pages as well as any news articles or company reviews. Share what interests you about the company's products, services, culture or values (you may also need to look at the specific division or team if interviewing at a large company).
Why are you interested in this position? Employers want to hire someone who will be happy and stay in the position, so they want to guage your interst level in that specific job opening. Review the job description and identify several responsibilities or factors that attract you to the role.
What are your strengths? Learning your strengths can help an employer envision the value you would bring to their team. Read through the job description to see what skills the company wants to see in a candidate. Match 2-3 of those skills with specific examples of how you've demonstrated that skill successfully in the past.
What are your weaknesses? Interviewers ask this question to gauge your self-awareness and ability to imporve Share one trait that dosen't affect your ability to do the core responsibilities of your job but that could use improvement. Describe your awareness of the trait and how you manage it or intend to improve upon it.
Why did you leave your last job? Employers wants to make sure that you have the traits to succeed and stay in the role. Be honest, but keep it concise and postive.
How do you prioritze your work? This question helps hiring managers evaluate your organizational skills and work ethics. Give an overview of the techniques, tools, or strategies you use to organize and priotize your work. If possible, give an example to illustrate your approach in action.
Describe how you deal with conflict in the workplace. This question helps the interviewer evaluate your conflict resolution skills and how you would operate in their team environment. Share a time that you successfully handled conflict in the workplace. Use the SAR method to succinctly describe the situation,, the action you took, and the result of your actions. Be careful not to speak too negatively about a coworker, supervisor, or company.
What's your preferred work environment/leadership style? Employers want to make sure you will thrive in their work environment. Be honest, concise, and positive - there is no right or wrong anser. You don't want to end up in the wrong work environmentwhere you won't be successful!
Why should we hire you over other qualified candidates? At the interview stage, all candidates are qualified, so this question can help employers determine who might eb the strongest fit for the position. Consider the unique experiences you have that may stand out to an employer. This could be specific industry knowledge, experience working in a similar environment, or training in a certain skill area. Also, consider the skills that make you stand out as an employee.
